Charlie AI — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 6, 2025

OVERVIEW

Charlie AI is a Chrome extension that helps you identify potentially unsafe or phishing websites by analyzing the current page's URL and visible text content. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data Charlie AI accesses, how it is used, and what we do not do.

DATA WE ACCESS

  • Current tab URL — used locally to evaluate risk signals (protocol, keywords, length).
  • Visible page text — scanned locally for suspicious patterns such as urgency language, credential requests, and phishing indicators.

DATA WE DO NOT COLLECT

  • We do not transmit any data to external servers.
  • We do not store your browsing history, URLs, or page content.
  • We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, or third-party services.
  • We do not collect personally identifiable information of any kind.

HOW ANALYSIS WORKS

All scanning happens entirely within your browser. The extension reads the active tab's URL and page content, applies heuristic rules locally, and displays the result in the popup. Nothing leaves your device.

PERMISSIONS

Charlie AI requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • activeTab — to read the URL of the current tab when you click the extension.
  • scripting / content scripts — to scan the visible text of the current page for risk indicators.

These permissions are used solely for on-device analysis as described above.

CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published here and the "Last updated" date will be revised. Continued use of the extension constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

CONTACT
